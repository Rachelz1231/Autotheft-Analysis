Introduction

Our purpose is to determine if the crime rate is the same in every division, if the proportion of crime increases over the years by comparing the proportions of crime in different years of a division, and the correlation between the number of crimes and three factors that we believe might affect the number of crimes (n_crimes). The three predictors (independent variables) we will be investigating will be n_25_to_54 (the number of people in a population ages 25 to 54), pop_2016 (the population in 2016), and n_elders (number of elders in a population). This will be done using the statistical methods of hypothesis testing, bootstrapping, confidence intervals, and linear regression using the data. The information resulting from these methods of statistical testing will be analyzed to determine which locations to station more security personnel.

Objectives

What kind of factors that may impact the crime rate in each Ontarios neighbourhood?

Bootstrap sampling is to estimate sampling distribution by re-sampling from the original sample. This is under the assumption that our sample is representative of the population To do so, we use R to draw 5000 bootstrap samples of size 133 from the original sample. For each bootstrap sample, we calculated the mean proportion of crime numbers in every neighbourhood of Ontario in 2018. In order to find an interval that contains the population parameter (that is the mean of crime number of Ontario neighbourhoods, we calculated the 90% confidence interval. It gives a range of plausible values for what the true parameter might be. We are 90% confident that the mean proportion of the average crime number in 2018 is between 96.48 and 113.92.

Conclusion

In conclusion, we see that crime rate is not the same in every division and it is especially high in the division D23, which means that more security personnel should be stationed to divisions that have a higher crime rate like D23. We also find that as population size increases, the crime rate generally increases and populations with more elderly are more likely to experience an increase in crime rate. Therefore, there should be more police in populated communities especially in those with a higher proportion of the elderly.
